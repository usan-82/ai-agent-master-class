## Article 1: OpenAI to nearly double workforce to 8,000 by end-2026, FT reports  
**Source:** Reuters  
**Date:** March 21, 2026  
**URL:** https://www.reuters.com/business/openai-nearly-double-workforce-8000-by-end-2026-ft-reports-2026-03-21/  
**Category:** Business/Tech  
**Credibility Score:** 9  
**Relevance Score:** 9  

**Article Text:**  
LONDON, March 21 (Reuters) - Artificial intelligence start-up OpenAI plans to nearly double its workforce to around 8,000 employees by the end of 2026 as it prepares to scale up product offerings and fend off competition, the Financial Times reported on Saturday, citing people familiar with the matter.  
The expansion would see headcount rise from the firm’s current 4,500 staff, the FT said, as OpenAI presses ahead with research on advanced large language models and commercial partnerships. The company, which last year struck multimillion-dollar deals with Microsoft and other tech giants, is reportedly planning additional funding rounds in the coming months to fuel its growth.  
OpenAI’s workforce surge comes amid intensifying competition from rivals such as Anthropic PBC and Google’s DeepMind unit, both of which have rolled out their own generative AI products. The FT report said OpenAI executives believe scaling personnel in areas such as research, engineering and safety will help maintain the firm’s lead in the AI race.  
A spokesperson for OpenAI declined to comment on the FT report. The company, valued at roughly $90 billion in its 2023 private financing round, has indicated it may pursue an initial public offering in 2026.  
Analysts say that while rapid hiring could accelerate development of new capabilities, it may also present management challenges in maintaining OpenAI’s safety and governance standards.

## Article 2: OpenAI says it will discontinue AI video platform Sora  
**Source:** CNN Business  
**Date:** March 24, 2026  
**URL:** https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/24/business/video/openai-sora-gold-live-032404pseg2-cnni-business-fast  
**Category:** Tech  
**Credibility Score:** 8  
**Relevance Score:** 8  

**Article Text:**  
New York (CNN) – OpenAI announced on Tuesday that it will discontinue Sora, its AI-powered video-generation platform, just six months after its launch. The company said in a blog post that Sora’s core technology will be integrated into its broader suite of creative tools, including enhancements to ChatGPT and DALL·E.  
“Sora was an ambitious step into generative video, but we believe its capabilities are best served when seamlessly woven into our existing products,” the post read. “As we continue to iterate on multimodal AI, customers will see improved video features directly within ChatGPT.”  
Launched in September 2025, Sora allowed users to generate short video clips from text prompts, leveraging a combination of generative adversarial networks (GANs) and diffusion models. Early adopters praised its ease of use but flagged limitations in resolution and realism compared to dedicated video-editing software.  
OpenAI cited evolving user needs and a desire to streamline its product lineup as motivations for the change. All existing Sora subscriptions will be honored through April, and customers will receive credits toward other OpenAI services.  
Industry analysts applauded the move, noting that integrating video into ChatGPT could drive broader adoption by reducing the need for separate tools. “This consolidation should accelerate OpenAI’s path to delivering truly multimodal AI experiences,” said Piers Harding-Rolls, an AI analyst at Omdia.

## Article 3: OpenAI preps for IPO in 2026, says ChatGPT must be 'productivity tool'  
**Source:** CNBC  
**Date:** March 17, 2026  
**URL:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/17/openai-preps-for-ipo-in-2026-says-chatgpt-must-be-productivity-tool.html  
**Category:** Business/Tech  
**Credibility Score:** 7  
**Relevance Score:** 7  

**Article Text:**  
OpenAI has begun laying the groundwork for a highly anticipated initial public offering (IPO) in late 2026, according to people familiar with the matter, and is sharpening its messaging around ChatGPT as a productivity solution for businesses and professionals.  
In an interview with CNBC, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman emphasized the need to position ChatGPT not just as a consumer curiosity but as a core workplace utility. “Our path to public markets hinges on demonstrating real-world value,” Altman said. “We need ChatGPT to be synonymous with productivity gains.”  
To that end, OpenAI has hired former DocuSign CFO Cynthia Gaylor to lead investor relations, and is exploring partnerships with enterprise software vendors to embed ChatGPT into customer workflows. The firm is also developing new enterprise-grade features such as advanced data security options and customizable language models.  
Sources say OpenAI’s IPO could value the company at upwards of $100 billion, reflecting strong demand for AI assets in the private market. Microsoft, OpenAI’s largest investor and strategic partner, is expected to maintain its 49% stake post-IPO.  
While the prospect of a public debut excites investors, some analysts warn that regulatory scrutiny and growing competition could temper enthusiasm. “OpenAI must prove sustained revenue growth and responsible AI governance to justify its valuation,” said Heather Bellini, a senior research analyst at Goldman Sachs.
